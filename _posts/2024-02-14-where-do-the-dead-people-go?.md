title: Where Do The Dead People Go?
---

I wonder where the dead people go? Do they carry any luggages with them? And what is their mode of transport? 

Growing up, my idea of death has undergone several transformations. I once read somewhere about death, and how, as we know it, it is not the opposite of life but rather a part of it, the ultimate conclusion. Since then I have never seen death in the same light I used to see it in before.

At a certain point in my life, I was so fascinated with the idea of death that I read almost every single philosopher's work on it and I thought I knew things.

When I was a child, I used to watch action movies and when I saw bullets being fired in them, I used to imagine myself in that situation and I would think, 'Oh how much of an idiot they're, why don't they just move away and dodge the bullet?'. Totally unaware of a bullet's velocity, the child me used to live happily in his bullet-resistive imagination. But one day experiencing the death of a person so close caught me by my collar and threw me out of that bullet-resistive imagination of mine.

Imagine this – you grow up watching someone be a part of your life, you laugh with them, go on walks together, watch movies on weekends and eat rasmalais. You see them smiling, crying, singing, dancing and experiencing every single possible human emotion and one day you wake up and find them lying on a 7 feet bed completely frozen in their position with so much calmness on their face that it starts messing up with your turbulent little head. You don't realize what's wrong, why the air around you is vibrating with the sobbing of familiar faces, but then the realization hits you, you feel it in your bones! You try to scream but your scream attenuates internally scratching your throat. And that's when you come to know what death is! 

I feel like people are born colorless, they get coloured over time with memories and experiences, good and bad. And each of those colors is reminiscent of people you made the memory with. 

I guess I will never know where the dead people go and if they're even allowed to carry their colors with them from the land of living but what I do know is the fact that their beautiful and vivid colors will never faint off me as long as I am alive and breathing.



*On experiencing death and loss*
